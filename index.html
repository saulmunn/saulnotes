Saul Notes

from Saul Munn

No new note knows well its place,
before it settles softly.

Green and golden summers,
give rise to reddish vines;
blued and whited winters,
no longer feel aligned.

These pages bleed with visions,
of wonderous hills behind.
Of gold and cherry countertops,
topped with ros√© wines.

My thoughts began to splinter,
and flitter,
and flicker,

and coalesce.
and attune.
and resonate.

No note knows not well its place,
not new nor known, not now.
None will settle softly;
none will settle softly.

No page will ever settle.
Never softly, hardly hardly.

About this site

Hi, I'm Saul. You can read about who I am here, read my more-polished writing here, or read my less-polished notes... well, here.

For this notebook, I drew inspiration from the notebooks of Michael Neilsen, Andy Matuschak, and David MacIver. If this notebook brings a tenth as joy to someone's life as any of theirs has to mine, I'll be thrilled. And a little worried for their sanity, but mostly thrilled. (I'd also love to meet them!)

In keeping with Andy Matuschak's tradition, there isn't a way to browse all of the links on this site. You'll need to discover them naturally, perhaps by recieving a link sent directly to you, or perhaps by following a trail of hyperlinks.
